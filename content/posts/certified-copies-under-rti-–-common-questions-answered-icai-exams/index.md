## 1. RTI Act mandates 30 days, but certified copies not received even after 30 days?

Yes. As per **Section 7(1) of the RTI Act, 2005**, information must be supplied **within 30 days** from the date of receipt of the RTI application. This period **includes Sundays and public holidays**.

However, this time an unusually large number of students applied for certified copies. As on date:

- More than **35,000 RTI applications** were filed with ICAI  
- Approximately **1,45,000+ answer sheets** (average 4 papers per student)  
- All answer sheets require **suo-motu verification**, scanning, and uploading  

Because of this volume, a **slight delay** may occur.

As per a **telephonic conversation with the CPIO of ICAI**, students are advised to:
- Wait for **3‚Äì5 additional days** after completion of 30 days  
- If still not received, **file a First Appeal on the RTI portal**
- Meanwhile, send a **reminder email** to the concerned CPIO mentioning the RTI application number

## 2. How to check the status of your RTI application?

You may check the status on the **RTI Online Portal**. However, it is **not necessary to check regularly**, as:

- You will receive **SMS and email alerts** when copies are hosted  
- You will also receive intimation when the RTI application is **disposed of**

---

## 3. Where are certified copies hosted?

Scanned copies of answer sheets are hosted either on:
- **ICAI Examination Portal**, or  
- http://cc.icaiexam.icai.org/account/login.aspx  

Login credentials will be sent to your **registered email ID**.

---

## 4. Download your certified copies at the earliest

Certified copies are generally available for **15 days from the date of hosting**.  
After that, the link **expires**.

‚û°Ô∏è **Download your copies immediately** once hosted.

## 5. Is suo-motu verification of marks carried out under RTI?

**Yes.**

ICAI carries out **suo-motu verification** under **Regulation 39(4) of the Chartered Accountants Regulations, 1988**. This is also explicitly mentioned in the email sent by ICAI while hosting the copies.

---

## 6. Should I separately apply for verification by paying ‚Çπ100 per paper?

**No.**

If you apply for certified copies under RTI:
- **Separate verification is not required**
- **No additional fee** of ‚Çπ100 per paper is payable
- Verification is done **free of cost** in the interest of students

## 7. I scored exactly 40 / 60 / 200 / 400 marks ‚Äî will my marks be reduced?

**No.**

As per the **High-Level Independent Committee Report dated 14 February 2020**, Page 49, Para 7.1:

> ICAI does **not reduce marks already awarded** during the process of verification.

If marks are **increased**, you will receive:
- Email intimation  
- **Revised mark sheet by Speed Post**

## 8. Discrepancy found in certified copies ‚Äî what should I do?

You may report discrepancies **only if covered under Regulation 39(4)**, such as:
- Totalling errors  
- Carry-forward errors  
- Unvalued questions or sub-parts  

‚ùå **Re-evaluation or re-examination is not permitted** under any circumstances.

If discrepancy exists:
- Submit a **written letter** clearly identifying the issue  
- Send by **Speed Post or Registered Post**  
- Within **30 days** from receipt of certified copies
